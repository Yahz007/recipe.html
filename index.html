Classic Homemade Lasagna

Rich ragù, creamy ricotta, and golden, bubbly cheese — detailed steps for reliable, delicious results.


Recipe Information:

    Course: Main; Prep and Cook Time: 1hr 30mins; Yield: 5-6 Servings; Difficulty: Beginner

Ingredients

For the Meat Sauce

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 500 g ground beef (about 1 lb)
  • 250 g ground pork (about 1/2 lb) (optional but adds richness)
  • 1 large yellow onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 800 g (28 oz) canned crushed tomatoes
  • 400 g (14 oz) tomato sauce
  • 1 cup water or beef broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/4 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped

For the Ricotta Mixture

  • 450 g (15 oz) ricotta cheese
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

For the Layers

  • 12 lasagna noodles (regular or no-boil)
  • 500 g shredded mozzarella (about 4 cups)
  • 1 cup freshly grated Parmesan

Step by step instructions

1) Make the Meat Sauce

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium heat.
  2. Add ground beef and pork, breaking it apart with a wooden spoon. Cook until browned (6–8 minutes). Drain excess fat if needed.
  3. Add onion and sauté until softened (about 4 minutes). Stir in garlic and cook 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Stir in tomato paste; cook 2 minutes to deepen flavor.
  5. Add crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, and water/broth. Stir well.
  6. Season with basil, oregano, red pepper flakes, salt, pepper, sugar, and parsley.
  7. Reduce heat to low and simmer uncovered for 20–30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until thickened.

2) Prepare the Ricotta Mixture

  1. In a medium bowl, combine ricotta, egg, parsley, salt, and pepper.
  2. Mix until smooth and well combined; set aside.

3) Cook the Lasagna Noodles

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
  2. Add noodles and cook until just al dente (slightly undercooked).
  3. Drain and lay noodles flat on parchment or lightly oiled sheets to prevent sticking.

4) Assemble the Lasagna

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom of a 9×13‑inch baking dish.
  3. Add the first layer of noodles (3–4 sheets, slightly overlapping).
  4. Spread 1/3 of the ricotta mixture evenly over the noodles.
  5. Sprinkle 1 cup mozzarella and 2–3 tablespoons Parmesan over the ricotta.
  6. Top with about 1 1/2 cups meat sauce.
  7. Repeat layers (noodles → ricotta → mozzarella & Parmesan → sauce) two more times.
  8. Finish with a final layer of noodles, remaining meat sauce, and the rest of the mozzarella & Parmesan.

5) Bake

  1. Cover the dish loosely with foil (tent so it doesn’t touch the cheese).
  2. Bake covered for 25 minutes.
  3. Remove foil and bake uncovered for an additional 20–25 minutes, until cheese is bubbly and golden brown.

6) Rest & Serve

  1. Let the lasagna rest for at least 15 minutes before slicing — this sets the layers.
  2. Garnish with fresh parsley or basil if desired and serve with a green salad and garlic bread.

Tips for success

  • Make ahead: Assemble up to 24 hours in advance; refrigerate, then bake when ready.
  • Freezing (unbaked): Wrap tightly and freeze. Bake from frozen at 350°F (175°C) for ~1 hour 20 minutes covered; uncover and bake until golden.
  • Cheese variety: Mix mozzarella with provolone or fontina for extra flavor depth.
  • Richer sauce: Add 1/2 cup red wine to the sauce after browning meat; simmer to reduce.
  • No‑boil noodles: Ensure sauce is slightly looser; add 1/4 cup extra liquid if needed.
  • Clean slices: Use a serrated knife and gently saw down through layers; a bench scraper works too.
